## The Shore Principle

**Minister to the minister first.**

People cannot give what they have not been given. To love others, we must first be loved. To minister, we must first be ministered to.

Before the app asks for action, it offers something:

| Instead of... | Something more like... |
|---------------|------------------------|
| "6 overdue items" | "Morning, President." |
| Red badges | A quiet moment |
| "You're behind" | "Here's one thing when you're ready" |
| Task lists that grow | Progress that fades away |
| Guilt | Grace |

The app should feel like:
- A warm fire after a long night
- Breakfast someone made for you
- A hand on your shoulder
- "Feed my sheep" ‚Äî as invitation, not command

## The Reflection Principle

**Remind them of the good they've done.**

Leaders often feel like they're on a treadmill ‚Äî always more to do, never finished. The list grows. The badges stay. The feeling: "I'm always behind."

But when they look back? They see something being built. One person at a time.

### What This Actually Sounds Like

Not metrics. Not dashboards. Just... remembering together.

**Personal:**
> "Remember the Garcias? They're in Primary now. You were part of that."

> "You sat with Bishop Martinez three times this quarter. That mattered."

> "Four families have new leaders because of conversations you had."

**The Stake:**
> "Seven brothers hold the Melchizedek Priesthood now who didn't in October."

> "Thirty-four people said yes to a calling this quarter. Thirty-four."

> "The talks at conference this year ‚Äî faith, family, service. Those came from somewhere."

**The Journey:**
> "Three months ago, this was just a name on a list. Now she's teaching the youth every Sunday."

> "That item you counseled about in August? It's done. A family is stronger because of it."

### When This Shows Up

- **Once in a while:** A quiet note ‚Äî "This week, two more people are serving."
- **Monthly:** A moment to look back ‚Äî not a report, just a reminder
- **Quarterly:** The fuller picture ‚Äî what you've been part of, by name when it matters
- **Yearly:** The whole story ‚Äî like flipping through a photo album of the year

### The Voice

Not: "You completed 47 tasks!"

More like: "47 people are serving now. You were part of the conversations that got them there."

Not: "12 interviews logged"

More like: "You sat with 12 leaders this quarter. You listened. That's not nothing."

## The Invisible Assistant

### The Best App Is No App

The goal isn't a better dashboard. The goal is no dashboard at all.

Administration should fade into the background until leaders forget it exists. Every feature should aim for its own obsolescence.

### What "Invisible" Actually Means

**Today:** You open the app, see tiles, pick one, drill in, act, repeat.

**Better:** You open the app, see one card, tap once, done.

**Best:** You get a notification, act from the lock screen, never open the app.

**Ideal:** You live your ministry. The system notices. Paperwork happens. You never think about it.

### The Sequence

1. **Awareness** ‚Äî "Here's what's going on"
2. **Prioritization** ‚Äî "Here's what matters most"
3. **Action** ‚Äî "Here ‚Äî do it now"
4. **Preparation** ‚Äî "I got it started for you"
5. **Anticipation** ‚Äî "You're going to need this"
6. **Invisible** ‚Äî "It's handled"
7. **Absence** ‚Äî "There is no app. There's just your ministry."

We're building toward 3-4 now. Each step earns the trust needed for the next.

## What We're Building Toward

Tap "Mark for Set Apart." Done. Never opened the app.

That's the future. Not today. But that's where we're headed.

## Design Principles

### Every Feature Should Ask:

1. **"Does this feel like the shore?"** ‚Äî Warm fire, breakfast, gentle invitation?

2. **"Does this minister before it asks?"** ‚Äî Does it give before it takes?

3. **"How does this disappear?"** ‚Äî What's the path to not needing it?

4. **"Does this clear the path for one sheep?"** ‚Äî Does it make space for that moment?

5. **"Would I feel lifted or burdened?"** ‚Äî Test it honestly on yourself.

### The Rhythm

1. Nourish first (a word, a memory, a breath)
2. Surface gently (one thing, when ready)
3. Enable action (one tap)
4. Acknowledge quietly (not applause, just recognition)
5. Return to silence

## What Success Looks Like

Not:
- App opens per week
- Time spent in app
- Features used
- Tasks completed

But:
- Did the leader have time for the person in front of them?
- Did they feel capable, not overwhelmed?
- Did they remember the good they've done?
- Did administration get out of the way?

It's about creating space.

Space for a Relief Society president to mourn with those who mourn.

We build the invisible assistant so that administration disappears and ministry remains.
